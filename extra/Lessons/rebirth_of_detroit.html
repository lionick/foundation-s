Rebirth of Detroit

Big Question

  1. Have current and previous plans to “improve” Detroit been an overall good or bad thing for the city and its residents?

Age level/grade:

  1. 4th grade

Summary (all subheadings optional/free text)

  1. Learning Objectives:
    1. Students will be able to identify a primary source v. a secondary source.
    2. Students will be able to analyze a photo or document and discuss public issues in Detroit.
  2. Standards:
    1. (Michigan) 4-P3.1.1 “Identify public issues in the United States that influence the daily lives of its citizens.”

Material Needed

  1. Pencils
  2. "items"
    1. View from Midtown House

Activities

  1. Intro: In this unit, students will explore the rebirth movement of Detroit after the years of civil unrest in the city, specifically the development during the years of 1967 to 1974. Many people were in agreement that the city needed to be improved, but the actions that were taken to do so were often controversial. Students will also be able to see the connections between 1950s-1960s urban renewal efforts to the current-day developments in the city.
  2. Class activities
    1. Ask the students what it means to analyze a photo or document to gather background knowledge.
    2. Review what a primary source is with the class.
    3. As a class, they will look at a photo called “VIEW FROM MIDTOWN HOUSE” and analyze the photo as a class.
    4. After discussing the photo, students will complete a worksheet on what they observed and what they inferred from the photo
    5. The class will come together and share what they wrote on their worksheets. They will also compare their observations with other students.
    6. Teacher will scroll through Image and select a few damage-related photos or images of children for students to ask about and discuss

Next Steps(optional)

  1. Students will be assessed by the work completed on their “What I observed/What I inferred” worksheet.
